## Mosaic 1: The Traffic Tax Explains Everything — Complaint Rates, Platform Switching, and Douyin's Rise

**Thesis:** The merchant cost structure gap (Q1) is the root cause of both the consumer complaint divergence (Q4) and Douyin's rapid shelf commerce adoption (Q5). These three findings, examined separately, look like independent trends. Together, they reveal a single mechanism: Taobao's traffic tax model is simultaneously degrading consumer experience and pushing merchants toward cheaper alternatives.

**Contributing data points:**
1. Taobao/Tmall merchants pay 20-40% of revenue all-in, with traffic/advertising alone consuming 10-30% of revenue and >50% of operating costs [E4][E12]
2. PDD merchants operating at best-case 5-7% all-in cost vs Taobao's 20-40% [E5]
3. Taobao's Heimao complaint response rate is under 10% — the worst of any major platform [E35][E36]
4. Douyin shelf commerce grew 49% YoY, hitting 50% of GMV by mid-2025 [E46]
5. A lamp factory owner reported promotion costs consuming 70% of profits, needing 400-500 RMB/day (up from 100 RMB) [E7]
6. Alibaba's CMR growth decelerated from 12% to 1% in four quarters [E1]
7. Douyin invested 32B yuan in merchant cost savings in 2025 [E14][E49]

**Why these converge:** When merchants must spend 10-30% of revenue on traffic just to be visible on Taobao, they cut corners elsewhere — customer service, product quality, fulfillment speed. This directly explains why Taobao's Heimao complaint response rate is under 10%: merchants who are barely profitable after paying for traffic cannot afford to staff complaint resolution teams. Meanwhile, Douyin's shelf commerce surge is not just about consumer behavior change — it represents merchants discovering that content-driven discovery replaces paid traffic. The 49% shelf GMV growth rate is merchants voting with their feet against pay-to-play.

**What the mosaic reveals that individual data points don't:** Alibaba's CMR deceleration from 12% to 1% is not a cyclical weakness — it is the early signal of a structural migration away from Taobao's traffic auction model. The complaint data (Q4) and the shelf commerce data (Q5) are two different measurements of the same underlying merchant exodus.

## Mosaic 2: The Food Delivery War Is an Attention War Disguised as a Logistics War

**Thesis:** The food delivery subsidy burn (Q2) and Douyin's commerce transformation (Q5) are connected by a shared strategic imperative: daily active user engagement. The food delivery war is not fundamentally about food — it is about which platform owns the consumer's daily routine.

**Contributing data points:**
1. Combined S&M spending: RMB 102.7B in Q2 2025 alone across three companies [E18]
2. Meituan's profit swing: 35.8B yuan profit (2024) to 24.3B yuan loss (2025) [E19]
3. Taobao's DAU outpaced Pinduoduo's by 100M+ during the "First Autumn Milk Tea" promotion on August 7 [E22]
4. Douyin DAU of ~766M and MAU exceeding 1 billion (from V1 data)
5. Douyin shelf commerce at 50% of GMV — converting attention into transactions [E46]
6. Alibaba's Taobao Flash Buy rose from near-zero to 35-37% market share [E22]

**Why these converge:** Alibaba is spending RMB 50B on food delivery not because food delivery itself is profitable — it never will be at current AOVs of RMB 50-65 with rider costs of RMB 7-9 per order [E24]. The real ROI is that food delivery creates daily app opens, which feed e-commerce transactions. The August 7 data point proves this: a milk tea promotion drove Taobao's DAU above Pinduoduo's, something that hadn't happened organically. Douyin already solved this problem differently — its content feed generates daily engagement that converts to commerce at 50% shelf rate. Alibaba is trying to replicate Douyin's engagement-to-commerce pipeline using food delivery instead of short video.

**What the mosaic reveals:** The RMB 100B+ food delivery burn is an investment in DAU, not in food logistics. Meituan is the collateral damage of a war between Alibaba and Douyin over daily consumer attention. JD's entry (5-8% share) is strategically incoherent — it lacks both the content engine (Douyin) and the local commerce ambition (Alibaba) to justify the investment.

## Mosaic 3: The Refund-Only Policy Reversal Marks the End of Consumer-Side Subsidy Wars

**Thesis:** The reversal of refund-only policies (Q4) combined with SAMR's fee regulation (Q1) and the food delivery regulatory interventions (Q2) mark a coordinated Beijing pivot from pro-consumer to pro-merchant-ecosystem policy. This reversal will reshape platform economics in 2026.

**Contributing data points:**
1. All major platforms reversed refund-only (仅退款) policies by July 2025 [E41]
2. One merchant reported 50% after-sales rate (20 refund-only + 80 returns out of 200 orders) [E41]
3. SAMR drafted 28-article guidelines requiring fee transparency, banning duplicate charging [E15]
4. SAMR summoned JD, Meituan, Ele.me in May 2025 over food delivery [E23]
5. State Council Anti-Monopoly Committee targeted food delivery "internal competition" in Jan 2026 [E23]
6. PDD Q1 2025 operating profit fell 38% YoY partly due to merchant support investments [E17]
7. PDD ranked #1 on Heimao "black list" in March 2025 — peak of consumer complaints [E40]

**Why these converge:** Refund-only was the consumer-side equivalent of food delivery subsidies — platforms competing by making returns frictionless at merchant expense. The 50% after-sales rate merchant example reveals why this was unsustainable. PDD pioneered refund-only in 2021, gained massive consumer share, but became #1 on Heimao's black list by 2025 as merchant quality deteriorated. The policy's reversal, combined with SAMR's fee regulations and food delivery interventions, represents a coordinated shift: Beijing concluded that excessive pro-consumer policies were destroying merchant viability and creating a race to the bottom.

**What the mosaic reveals:** The regulatory pivot from pro-consumer to pro-ecosystem is the single most underappreciated catalyst for Chinese e-commerce margins in 2026. Every subsidy war — food delivery, refund-only, merchant fees — is being curtailed simultaneously. This is structurally bullish for platform profitability across the industry.
