# Xinpinmu: The Costco Thesis Eight Years in the Making

*Published March 28, 2026 | Deep Research Report*

## Executive Thesis

On June 30, 2018, Huang Zheng wrote in his IPO letter that Pinduoduo would become "a Costco and Disney driven by distributed intelligent agent networks." [E1] Eight years later, PDD is building the Costco half.

Xinpinmu (新拼姆) is a self-operated brand initiative announced March 25, 2026. RMB 15B initial injection, RMB 100B committed over three years. Two Shanghai entities incorporated. The plan: curate ~4,000 branded SKUs manufactured by China's industrial clusters and distribute them globally through Temu's 90-country network. [E2] [E3]

Duan Yongping — BBK/VIVO founder, Huang Zheng's mentor, holder of $906M in PDD shares — gave the clearest articulation of what this means on Xueqiu: *"This is essentially the Costco model. If PDD's online brand has around 4,000 SKUs, in 10 years it will be quite formidable."* [E4]

More importantly, he described the operating model: *"PDD doesn't need to steal merchants' business — just nurture hit-product companies, supervise quality, and license its brand to qualified factories."* [E5] This is not a private-label-competitor play. It is a brand-licensing model: PDD owns the brand, certifies quality, and authorizes production to factories that meet its standards. The factory profits from volume and brand access. PDD profits from brand premium and platform stickiness.

We believe Xinpinmu is strategically necessary (Temu's cross-border model is dying under tariffs), structurally sound (brand-licensing is lighter than the distributor model most analysts are comparing it to), and currently priced at zero in PDD's stock. The execution risk is real but the asymmetry is favorable.

## The Founding Vision

Xinpinmu did not emerge from a vacuum. It traces directly to PDD's founding DNA.

Huang Zheng's IPO letter described a "Costco + Disney" platform — multi-value (多实惠) plus multi-fun (多乐趣). He admitted the Disney half was nascent: *"We want to build Disneyland, but we've only built a merry-go-round."* [E1] By 2023, PDD had quietly shifted positioning to "multi-value + good service" (多实惠 + 好服务), deprioritizing Disney in favor of operational excellence. [E14]

The Costco half took longer to build because it required something PDD didn't have in 2018: deep manufacturing cluster relationships. PDD's C2M (consumer-to-manufacturer) model evolved through four phases:

| Phase | Period | What PDD Did | What It Built |
|-------|--------|-------------|---------------|
| 1 | 2018 | New Brand Plan 1.0 — 1,000 factory partners | Initial factory network |
| 2 | 2020 | Plan 2.0 — 5,000 partners, 百亿补贴 | Scale + consumer trust in value |
| 3 | 2022-2025 | Temu + 新质供给 embedded teams | International distribution + 100+ cluster relationships |
| 4 | 2026 | Xinpinmu — PDD owns the brand | Brand ownership on top of existing infrastructure |

[E15] [E16]

Each phase built capabilities for the next. By Phase 3, PDD had embedded specialized teams in 100+ manufacturing clusters through the "新质供给" (New Quality Supply) program. The results were measurable: average order value increased 47%, repeat purchase rate rose from 23% to 40%. [E17]

Xinpinmu is not PDD learning to work with factories. PDD already knows how. Xinpinmu is PDD deciding to own the brand.

## How It Works: The Kirkland Model

### The Operating Model

Kirkland Signature is the gold standard for what Xinpinmu aspires to become. $58-90B in annual sales from roughly 600 SKUs — 23% of Costco's revenue from just 12.5% of its assortment. [E13] [E25]

The operational details matter:

**Costco does not own factories.** It contracts with premium national-brand manufacturers. Starbucks roasts Kirkland coffee. Duracell makes Kirkland batteries. Kimberly-Clark produces Kirkland diapers. The factory produces for both the national brand and Kirkland simultaneously — dual manufacturing is the norm, not the exception. [E26]

**Quality is non-negotiable.** Costco's CEO personally signs off on each new Kirkland item. Products must match or exceed national brand quality at 20% lower prices. Categories where this standard cannot be met are abandoned entirely — Kirkland does not sell cosmetics, soda, or toothpaste. [E27]

**Pricing is disciplined.** Maximum 14% markup on national brands, 15% on Kirkland. The margin premium comes from cutting out brand marketing costs, not from charging more. Kirkland margins run 5-8 percentage points above national brands. [E28] [E13]

**Trust compounds over decades.** Thirty years of consistent quality delivery. Members trust Kirkland because Costco has never compromised. The result: 33.7% ROIC versus Walmart's 17.1%, and $850K revenue per employee versus $348K industry average. [E30]

### Xinpinmu's Adaptation

Xinpinmu adapts Kirkland for a digital-native platform:

- **Curation**: ~4,000 branded SKUs (Duan Yongping's number), covering approximately 80% of high-frequency household consumption. [E31] Costco runs ~3,700 active SKUs. This is radical curation — the opposite of PDD's current model with millions of SKUs from millions of merchants.
- **Brand licensing**: Factories produce to PDD's specifications under the Xinpinmu brand. PDD certifies quality, owns the brand, handles distribution. Lighter than Kirkland (which buys and holds inventory) because factories ship direct or to Temu warehouses.
- **Global from day one**: Kirkland expanded internationally over decades. Xinpinmu launches into 90+ countries through Temu's existing infrastructure — warehouses, logistics, traffic.
- **Data advantage**: PDD has purchase data on 872M domestic users and Temu's global user base. Kirkland relied on buyer intuition. Xinpinmu can algorithmically identify which products to brand.

### Three Models, One Label

The term "self-operated" (自营) is used loosely in Chinese e-commerce, but it describes three structurally different businesses:

| | JD Self-Operated | Amazon Basics | Xinpinmu / Kirkland |
|-|-----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **What they do** | Buy Samsung TVs, resell | Create batteries that undercut Duracell | Design specs, license brand to factories |
| **Brand** | Sells others' brands | Owns competing brand | Owns complementary brand |
| **Inventory** | Heavy — buys and holds | Medium | Light — factory produces to order |
| **Gross margin** | 14-19% | ~30% est. | 20-35% |
| **Merchant conflict** | High | High (FTC trial Oct 2026) | Low by design |

[E6] [E7] [E9]

The distinction is critical for modeling. JD's self-operated business runs at 14-19% gross margin because JD is a distributor — buying branded inventory and reselling at thin markups. Xinpinmu is a brand owner. The correct margin benchmarks are brand-owner comparables:

| Company | Model | Gross Margin |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Anker Innovations | Chinese DTC brand | 44.6% |
| UGREEN | Chinese e-commerce brand | 37.4% |
| Amazon 3P private label | Brand-owner on marketplace | 30-50% |
| Xiaomi IoT/Lifestyle | Platform ecosystem brand | 23-25% |
| Kirkland Signature | Curated, price-capped | ~15% |

[E10] [E11] [E12] [E13] [E44]

Xinpinmu's margin will depend on pricing philosophy. Priced aggressively like Kirkland: ~20%. Positioned like Xiaomi ecosystem: ~25%. Premium like Anker: ~35%. The realistic modeling range is 20-30%.

## The Merchant Question

### Complementary by Design

Duan Yongping's interpretation of the merchant relationship is the key insight most analysis misses:

> *"PDD doesn't need to steal merchants' business — just nurture hit-product companies, supervise quality, and license its brand to qualified factories. It only needs to select certain representative products to build its own brand."* [E5]

This describes a **per-SKU authorization model**: Xinpinmu handles standardized products (标品) — the commodity items where brand trust matters and individual merchant differentiation is minimal. Existing merchants handle non-standard, personalized, and long-tail products (非标) — where human curation, niche expertise, and creative merchandising create real value. [E32]

The framework is structural division of labor, not zero-sum competition.

Kirkland validates this. National brand manufacturers simultaneously produce their own branded products and Kirkland products. Starbucks, Duracell, and Kimberly-Clark all operate this dual model profitably. Factory volumes increase. The manufacturer's branded product may face modest pricing pressure on specific SKUs, but total output grows. [E26]

### The 新质供给 Foundation

Xinpinmu builds on relationships PDD has already established. The "New Quality Supply" (新质供给) program has been running since April 2025, with teams embedded in 100+ manufacturing clusters:

- **Shaodong luggage**: Produces 70%+ of China's student backpacks. PDD's digital support helped "Cooleni" brand emerge from pure OEM production.
- **Weihai fishing gear**: 60%+ of the world's fishing rods. Handing brand already doing RMB 300M+ on Amazon. First regional collective trademark in Chinese fishing gear.
- **Yiwu cosmetics**: 170+ factories. Chinese cosmetics exports +12% YoY in H1 2025.
- **Pinghu down jackets**: 2,000+ factories, RMB 50B sales, 80% national share. Transitioning from OEM to design-led production.

[E17]

These are factories that want platform-backed brand support. They have world-class manufacturing but lack marketing expertise, international certification, and brand development resources. Xinpinmu provides exactly what they're missing.

### The Honest Tension

The complementary framing is the design intent. Execution will introduce real tension:

- Even Kirkland displaces national brands — Kind bars, Planters nuts, and Pampers have all lost shelf space to Kirkland equivalents. Lululemon sued Costco in 2025 over a Kirkland lookalike. [E33]
- On standardized commodity products, Xinpinmu will take share from existing PDD merchants selling unbranded versions. [E34]
- PDD's traffic algorithm could preference Xinpinmu products — the "referee and athlete" concern that triggered the FTC's antitrust suit against Amazon. [E36]

The question is whether overall ecosystem value grows — factory volumes up, consumer trust up, platform stickiness up — enough to offset category-level displacement. Costco's track record suggests yes. But PDD is attempting this transition faster and across a more complex marketplace.

## Why Now: The Tariff Survival Engine

### The Cross-Border Model Is Dead

Xinpinmu is not just a growth strategy. It is a survival mechanism for Temu.

**United States**: De minimis exemption permanently revoked for China/Hong Kong on May 2, 2025. A $100 per-parcel postal fee now applies to every shipment. [E20] The math for a $10 product:

| | Old Temu Model | Xinpinmu Model |
|-|---------------|----------------|
| Product cost | $10.00 | $10.00 |
| Shipping | $7.00 (air parcel) | $0.50 (bulk ocean) |
| Tariff | $5.40 (54%) | $2.50 (25% standard) |
| Per-parcel fee | $100.00 | $0 (bulk entry) |
| Warehouse/pick | $0 | $3.00 |
| **Landed cost** | **$122.40** | **$16.00** |

[E37] [E39]

The $100 per-parcel fee is the killer. It makes any sub-$100 product uneconomical via individual cross-border parcels.

**European Union**: EUR 3 flat customs duty per item on all parcels under EUR 150 from July 1, 2026. EUR 2 additional handling fee from November 2026. Full standard tariffs from 2028. 4.6 billion shipments per year affected, 91% from China. [E38]

**Impact**: Temu US daily users fell 48% in May 2025. App ranking crashed from #3 to #85 in two weeks. Low-value parcel volume dropped 85% — from 4 million daily to 600,000. [E20]

### The New Value Chain

Xinpinmu restructures Temu from a cross-border parcel shipper into a local-warehouse retailer with owned brands:

**Factory** → Xinpinmu quality certification → PDD branding/packaging → **bulk ocean container** ($0.10-0.30/kg vs $6-10/parcel air) → **formal customs entry** (standard tariff, no per-parcel fee) → **local Temu warehouse** (13 self-owned: 10 EU, 2 US, 1 Mexico) → **local carrier delivery** (2-5 days vs 7-15 days)

Temu itself reports 40-60% logistics cost reduction from bulk shipping to UK warehouses alone. [E41] The EU target is 80% local fulfillment by end of 2026. [E40]

### The New Costs

This is not a free lunch. PDD is taking on costs it has never borne:

- **Inventory risk**: PDD was a pure marketplace with zero inventory. Xinpinmu requires pre-purchasing and holding stock in overseas warehouses. [E42]
- **Working capital**: RMB 15B first tranche is real cash deployed, not platform commissions earned. [E2]
- **Brand infrastructure**: Product design, packaging, IP protection, quality certification, regulatory compliance across 90+ countries. [E43]
- **Organizational build**: PDD's 17,000-person workforce must add product designers, quality inspectors, warehouse operators, brand marketers, and IP lawyers. [E42]

Chinese media describes this as "搬砖类型的苦活累活" — heavy brick-carrying work. [E42] It is a fundamental departure from PDD's asset-light DNA. The company that generated 70-78% ROIC on an asset-light model is deliberately making itself heavier.

The question is whether the brand premium captured justifies the operational weight added. Higher-value branded products absorb tariffs more easily — a $12 branded fishing rod absorbs a 25% tariff better than a $5 generic one — and they generate higher absolute margins per unit. [E44]

### Scenario Analysis

| Scenario | Gross Margin | Revenue (Year 5) | Operating Profit | Accretive? |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Bull | 30% (Anker-lite) | RMB 50B | ~RMB 5B | Yes — meaningful |
| Base | 22% (Xiaomi ecosystem) | RMB 30B | ~RMB 1.5B | Yes — modest |
| Bear | 15% (Kirkland pricing) | RMB 15B | Breakeven | No — margin drag |

[E10] [E11] [E12] [E45]

Xinpinmu becomes clearly accretive at approximately 22% gross margin with RMB 25B+ revenue. Below that, it compresses blended margins without creating sufficient profit to justify the investment.

If Xinpinmu reaches 10% of PDD's revenue at 22% gross margin, company-wide blended gross margin drops from ~57% to ~53%. This looks like compression — but margin percentage is not the same as value. Amazon's gross margin compressed as AWS scaled; the stock quadrupled. Revenue quality matters: branded product revenue is more durable than advertising revenue, which depends on merchants bidding for traffic.

### Option Value

The market assigns Xinpinmu approximately zero value today:

- PDD enterprise value: ~$85B for $15.4B net income = 5.6x EV/NI [E46]
- Cash: RMB 422.3B = 56% of market cap [E21]
- 10.5x P/E despite 70-78% ROIC — deep value multiple reflecting capital allocation skepticism [E46]

If Xinpinmu generates $3-5B annual operating profit at maturity, it would be worth $30-75B at 10-15x earnings — representing 35-86% upside to current enterprise value. [E47]

The asymmetry: downside is manageable margin compression (RMB 100B = 24% of cash, fundable from FCF). Upside is a new branded business with global distribution, currently priced at zero.

## The Central Tension: 3 Years vs. 10 Years

Duan Yongping says 10 years. Management says 3. This is the most important tension in the entire thesis.

Costco spent its first decade (1983-1993) not expanding aggressively but polishing ~4,000 SKUs — compressing costs, stabilizing quality, building trust. Duan's 10-year framework maps to this: *"It's not about the time itself but emphasizing that quality business models need time to be forged."* [E54]

Management's three-year, RMB 100B commitment reflects urgency — the tariff crisis is forcing Temu's model transformation now, not in a decade.

The resolution may be that RMB 100B over three years builds the **infrastructure** — factory relationships, warehouse network, quality systems, initial SKU portfolio — while brand trust accumulates over the subsequent seven years. The investment front-loads. The returns back-load.

Investors should judge Xinpinmu on infrastructure milestones at year 3 and brand outcomes at year 10.
