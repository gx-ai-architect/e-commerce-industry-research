# Xinpinmu: PDD's Hundred-Billion-Dollar Bet on Becoming China's Costco

*Published March 28, 2026 | Deep Research Report*

## Executive Thesis

Three days ago, PDD Holdings announced its most consequential strategic pivot since the launch of Temu: a self-operated brand initiative called "Xinpinmu" (新拼姆), backed by RMB 15B in initial capital and a three-year commitment of RMB 100B. The company that built the world's fastest-growing e-commerce platform on asset-light marketplace commissions now wants to design, manufacture, and sell its own products globally. [E1]

This is not a minor product extension. It is a fundamental business model inversion — from referee to player, from platform to retailer, from 56% gross margins to potentially 15%. Every Chinese internet platform that has attempted this transition has either failed (Alibaba's Taobao Xinxuan, shut down 2022; Maoxiang, shut down 2023) or stalled (NetEase Yanxuan, declining revenue). [E7] [E8] PDD itself tried a self-operated store called "Fulishe" in 2023 — it was shut down within four hours after merchant backlash. [E25]

Yet PDD has three assets no predecessor possessed: Temu's distribution network across 90+ countries, RMB 422B in cash, and the deepest manufacturing cluster relationships in Chinese e-commerce. [E11] [E23] The question is whether these advantages are sufficient to overcome the structural headwinds that have defeated every prior attempt.

Our assessment: **Xinpinmu is more likely to succeed internationally (via Temu) than domestically (via Pinduoduo), and the three-year timeline is unrealistically aggressive.** The initiative makes strategic sense as a response to trade barriers that threaten Temu's cross-border model, but the capital allocation math is demanding and the merchant ecosystem risk is real.

## What Xinpinmu Is

On March 25, 2026, PDD announced Xinpinmu alongside its FY2025 earnings. Co-CEO Zhao Jiazhen described it as "recreating another Pinduoduo" through self-operated brands. [E78]

## The Precedent Problem

### Every Chinese Platform Has Failed at This

The track record of Chinese internet platforms attempting private labels is unambiguous:

| Initiative | Platform | Launched | Status | What Happened |
|-----------|----------|----------|--------|---------------|
| Taobao Xinxuan | Alibaba | 2017 | **Shut down** (2022) | Couldn't sell clothing/food to avoid competing with advertisers; restructured 3 times in 5 years |
| Maoxiang (猫享) | Alibaba | 2022 | **Shut down** (2023) | Lasted one year |
| Tao Yanxuan | Alibaba | 2025 | **Quality issues** | Cotton product tested at 4.8% cotton content |
| NetEase Yanxuan | NetEase | 2016 | **Declining** | FY2025 segment revenue RMB 6.8B; missed 2018 target of RMB 20B GMV |
| JD Jingzao | JD.com | 2018 | **Surviving** | 600+ categories, 200M+ users, but revenue undisclosed; JD says "not seeking brand premium" |
| Fulishe | PDD | 2023 | **Shut down** (4 hours) | Merchant backlash forced immediate closure |

[E7] [E8] [E6] [E117] [E5] [E25]

The pattern is clear: marketplaces that try to become retailers face internal organizational resistance, merchant conflict, and an inability to sustain quality at scale.

### The Costco Analogy Is Misleading

PDD and Chinese media have drawn parallels to Costco's Kirkland Signature, which generated $90B in sales in FY2025 — roughly 33% of Costco's $270B in merchandise revenue. [E12] Kirkland margins run 5-8 percentage points above national brands, and it has become the largest CPG brand in America by sales, surpassing Nike and Pepsi. [E14] [E16]

But the analogy breaks down on a critical structural point: **Costco was already a retailer when it launched Kirkland.** It owned stores, managed inventory, controlled the shelf, and curated a limited SKU assortment. Adding private label was a natural extension of existing capabilities. [E10]

PDD is a marketplace trying to become a retailer — the opposite direction. It has never managed inventory, controlled product quality at scale, or operated physical supply chains. Its entire organizational DNA is built around connecting buyers to merchants, not replacing merchants. [E10] [E20]

Moreover, Kirkland took 30 years to reach $90B. PDD is planning a three-year timeline. [E13]

### Amazon Is Retreating

The other major precedent — Amazon Basics — is also cautionary. Amazon cut its private-label brands from 45 to fewer than 20, and private labels now represent less than 1% of US retail revenue, declining year over year. [E2] [E1]

The reason is instructive: the trust and regulatory cost exceeded the revenue benefit. The FTC antitrust trial, set for October 2026, specifically alleges Amazon self-preferenced its own products in search results. Congressional lawmakers accused Amazon of "possibly lying" about using seller data for private labels. [E3] [E28]

Amazon retreated from private labels despite them being <1% of revenue. The lesson: **the damage to platform trust from competing with your own merchants can exceed any margin benefit from self-operated brands.**

## The Merchant Dilemma

### 100% Revenue Dependency on Third-Party Merchants

This is the central tension of Xinpinmu: **PDD's entire RMB 431.8B revenue comes from third-party merchants.** [E41] There is no other revenue source. Online marketing services (merchant advertising) and transaction services (merchant commissions) are both functions of merchant activity on the platform. [E43]

PDD has 14.2 million active merchants generating 55.3 million jobs. [E42] Self-operated brands would compete directly with these merchants for traffic, category share, and consumer attention — the same merchants that fund PDD's entire operation.

### Zero Merchant Protection Policies

Across all official Xinpinmu announcements — from Xinhua to 36kr to Sina Finance — **there is no mention of traffic allocation protections, category restrictions for self-operated brands, merchant advisory councils, or any competitive safeguard for existing sellers.** [E48]

PDD's "Thousand Billion Support" plan from April 2025 addresses merchant welfare broadly but does not specifically address Xinpinmu competition. [E39] The silence on this topic is itself a data point: either PDD has not thought through the conflict, or it has decided not to make commitments that would constrain Xinpinmu's growth.

### The JD Warning

JD's self-operated business provides the clearest preview of what PDD merchants can expect:

| Metric | JD Self-Operated | JD 3P Marketplace |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|
| GMV share | 27% | 73% |
| Homepage display positions | 6-8 | 2-3 |
| Conversion rate | 18% | ~7% |
| Search CTR advantage | +40% with "JD Self-Operated" label | Baseline |

[E31] [E32]

Industry insiders describe JD as "both referee and athlete" — POP merchants cannot truly compete with self-operated products that receive systematic traffic advantages. [E33] JD had to launch its RMB 350B "Spring Dawn Plan" in March 2026 specifically to compensate third-party merchants for the disadvantages of competing against JD's own products. [E35]

### A Platform Already Under Stress

The merchant ecosystem risk is amplified by PDD's weakening user base. **PDD is the only top-10 internet platform in China with declining users — down 10.6% year over year as of December 2025.** [E37] The price-driven traffic algorithm has created what merchants describe as a "cost compression → quality compromise" cycle, where false eyelash producers' per-box profit fell from RMB 0.80 to RMB 0.15. [E36]

Multi-platform operation ("PDD + Douyin + Tmall") is now mainstream, giving merchants exit options they didn't have two years ago. [E38] Introducing self-operated brands into this already-stressed ecosystem is adding fuel to a fire.

The contradicting evidence: young merchant growth remains strong (post-95 merchants +31% YoY, post-00 merchants +44%). [E40] PDD is still attracting new sellers even as existing ones face margin pressure.

## The Capital Allocation Math

### PDD Can Afford It — But Should It?

PDD's financial capacity to fund Xinpinmu is not in question:

| Metric | FY2025 | Context |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Cash + short-term investments | RMB 422.3B | +27% YoY |
| Operating cash flow | RMB 106.9B | Funds the initiative from cash flow alone |
| Revenue | RMB 431.8B | +10% YoY |
| Net income | RMB 99.4B | -12% YoY (first meaningful decline) |
| Capex | <RMB 1B | <1% of revenue — extreme asset-light |
| ROIC | 70-78% | Among highest globally |

[E58] [E59] [E23] [E69]

RMB 100B over three years is approximately 31% of projected three-year free cash flow — significant but manageable without touching the existing cash pile. [E60]

### The ROI Hurdle Is Demanding

The math of making Xinpinmu work is challenging:

**At a 15% ROI target** (above PDD's ~10.5% WACC), Xinpinmu would need to generate approximately RMB 15B in annual operating profit at steady state. [E72]

| Margin Assumption | Revenue Required | % of PDD FY2025 Revenue |
|-------------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| JD-like 6% operating margin | RMB 250B | 58% |
| Kirkland-like 8% operating margin | RMB 188B | 44% |
| Below-JD 5% operating margin | RMB 300B | 70% |

[E72] [E73]

For context: JD built its self-operated business over 20+ years with tens of billions in logistics infrastructure investment. [E73] Costco's Kirkland took 30 years to reach $90B. PDD is attempting to build a comparable operation from zero in three years.

### Margin Compression Is Already Happening

PDD's gross margin declined from 60.9% to 56.3% in a single year — the first significant compression in company history — **before Xinpinmu even launches.** [E63] Self-operated retail typically runs at 14-19% gross margins (JD's benchmark). [E55] Every dollar shifted from marketplace commissions to self-operated revenue will carry dramatically lower margins.

The market appears to be pricing in this concern: PDD trades at 10.5x P/E and 5.4x EV/FCF despite 70-78% ROIC — a deep value multiple that suggests capital allocation skepticism. [E70]

### The Bright Spot

China's private label market is in a "golden growth phase" — FMCG private label share reached 2% of sales in Q3 2025, growing 44% over two years. [E74] Compared to global markets where private labels command 15-40% share, the runway is enormous. If Xinpinmu captures even 5% of PDD's estimated domestic GMV (~RMB 4T), self-operated revenue would reach RMB 175-225B, potentially meeting the ROI hurdle at Kirkland-type margins. [E75]

## The International Pivot: Why Xinpinmu Is Really About Temu

### The Tariff Crisis Made This Necessary

The strongest case for Xinpinmu is not the domestic market — it's Temu. The cross-border model that powered Temu's explosive growth has been demolished by trade policy:

- **US**: De minimis exemption permanently revoked for China/HK in May 2025, then globally in August 2025. Low-value parcel volume dropped 85% — from 4 million daily to 600,000. [E99]
- **EU**: Flat EUR 3 customs duty per parcel on all sub-EUR 150 imports from July 1, 2026. Full tariffs from 2028. 4.6 billion shipments under EUR 150 entered the EU in 2024, 91% from China. [E100]
- **Impact**: Temu US daily users fell 48% in May 2025, app ranking crashed from #3 to #85 in two weeks. [E101]

Xinpinmu with local warehousing fundamentally changes the equation. Instead of per-parcel cross-border shipping (which triggers per-unit tariffs), owned brands can be bulk-imported into local warehouses and fulfilled domestically — avoiding the worst tariff impacts. Temu already operates 13 self-owned warehouses globally (10 EU, 2 US, 1 Mexico). [E95] [E102]

### The Brand Perception Problem

Temu's brand perception is a liability. Trustpilot: 2.2/5. Sitejabber: 2/5. 42% of consumers believe Temu quality is worse than US retailers. [E84] Despite 86% brand awareness, only 24% of users make repeat purchases. [E83]

Owned brands with quality control could address this — similar to how Amazon Basics initially helped legitimize Amazon's marketplace. [E88] But App Store ratings tell a different story: Temu holds a 4.7/5 rating across US and European markets. [E85] The quality perception gap may be driven more by brand positioning ("it's too cheap to be good") than actual product quality.

### Competitive Differentiation

Xinpinmu would differentiate Temu from every major competitor:

| Platform | Model | Private Label | Weakness Xinpinmu Addresses |
|----------|-------|---------------|----------------------------|
| Shein | Hybrid (65% private label, 35% marketplace) | Core business | Temu has no brand DNA — Xinpinmu adds it |
| AliExpress | Marketplace + Choice | Xiaomi partnership only | No systematic brand development |
| Amazon Haul | Low-cost storefront | Minimal | Underperforming (16% monthly purchase vs Temu 28%) |

[E89] [E90] [E91]

Nomura upgraded PDD to Buy specifically citing that Xinpinmu helps Temu cope with regulatory scrutiny and tariff barriers. [E106]

PDD's Xinpinmu teams have visited 100+ manufacturing clusters. [E110] Our research evaluated six named clusters:

| Shaodong Luggage | Bags/backpacks | RMB 26B+, 70% of China's student backpacks | **MEDIUM** | Volume +1.6% but value -13.5% in 2025 — the smiling curve in action [E112] [E131] |
| Shandong Snacks | Food | RMB 800B domestic market | **LOW** | Almost no export data; FDA/safety certifications are complex barrier [E122] [E123] |

[E110] [E111]

### The Anker Standard

Anker is the gold standard for Chinese OEM-to-brand transitions: founded 2011, now RMB 29.3B TTM revenue with a $9.4B market cap. But Anker invested 50% of staff in R&D, built an IP portfolio of 2,200+ patents, and spent 14 years reaching its current scale. [E108] [E109]

PDD's three-year timeline assumes it can replicate across hundreds of categories what Anker achieved in one category over 14 years. The Xiaomi ecosystem offers a partial template — 400 invested companies, ~330 still active — but successful companies tend to "escape" the platform for higher margins. Huami's Xiaomi revenue share dropped from 92% to below 50%. [E116]

### The Smiling Curve Trap

The "smiling curve" problem is real and measurable. Chinese manufacturing sits at the lowest point of the value curve, capturing 3-10% margins on products whose R&D (left end) and branding/distribution (right end) command 20-40% margins. [E127]

Moving up the curve requires R&D investment, design capability, brand marketing, and — critically — international regulatory compliance. CE and FDA certifications have shifted from competitive differentiators to mandatory entry barriers. Small OEM factories typically lack dedicated certification teams. [E128]

China's Made in China 2025 initiative broadly succeeded at the sector level for industrial manufacturing, but consumer brand building operates under different dynamics. [E129] Digital infrastructure is improving (70%+ of large manufacturers completed digital networking by 2025), but digital capability does not equal brand capability. [E130]

## Investment Implications

### What This Means for the Stock

Xinpinmu is a **margin-dilutive, strategically necessary pivot** that will take longer than management suggests and cost more than the market expects.

**Bull case**: Xinpinmu cracks the code on OEM-to-brand for Temu's international market, creating a differentiated retailer with Kirkland-like margins. At 5% GMV penetration and 8% operating margins, it generates RMB 15B+ annual operating profit. PDD re-rates from 10x to 15x P/E. Upside: 50%+.

**Base case**: Xinpinmu achieves modest success in select categories (fishing gear, 3C electronics) and geographies (EU, Southeast Asia) but struggles with food, fashion, and the US market. Domestic Pinduoduo merchants grumble but don't leave en masse because alternatives aren't better. Margin compression accelerates. Revenue growth stabilizes at mid-single digits. Stock treads water at 10-12x P/E.

**Bear case**: Merchant backlash triggers an exodus similar to Fulishe but at scale. PDD's user decline accelerates. Regulatory scrutiny follows the Amazon antitrust playbook — Chinese regulators have already banned platform price coercion (effective October 2025 and April 2026). [E19] RMB 100B produces nothing comparable to Temu's initial return. Stock de-rates to 7-8x P/E.

### Kill Conditions

Three observable events that would invalidate the thesis:

1. **Merchant count declines >10% in two consecutive quarters** after Xinpinmu launch — signals ecosystem rejection
2. **Gross margin falls below 40%** within 18 months — indicates self-operated economics are worse than JD's and the margin compression is structural
3. **Xinpinmu brands receive systematic traffic preference** visible in search results — would trigger regulatory action under new Chinese anti-price-coercion rules and echo Amazon's antitrust problems

### What to Watch

- **Q1 2026 earnings (May 2026)**: First quarter with Xinpinmu operational. Look for merchant count trends, category-level data, and management commentary on cannibalization.
- **FTC vs. Amazon trial (October 2026)**: Ruling will set precedent for platform private-label antitrust globally, including potential Chinese regulatory follow-on.
- **EU EUR 3 duty implementation (July 2026)**: First test of whether Xinpinmu's bulk-import model actually works at scale.
- **Manufacturing cluster output**: Track whether Xinpinmu brands appear on Temu in US/EU with quality ratings and reviews.
