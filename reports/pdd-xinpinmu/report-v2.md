# Xinpinmu: The Costco Thesis Eight Years in the Making

*Published March 28, 2026 | Deep Research Report V2*

## Executive Thesis

On June 30, 2018, Huang Zheng wrote in his IPO letter that Pinduoduo would become "a Costco and Disney driven by distributed intelligent agent networks." Eight years later, PDD is finally building the Costco half. [E1]

Xinpinmu (新拼姆) — announced March 25, 2026 alongside FY2025 earnings — is not a random pivot or a defensive reaction. It is the fulfillment of PDD's founding vision: a curated, brand-licensed product line of ~4,000 SKUs built from China's manufacturing clusters and distributed globally through Temu's 90-country network. [E2] [E3]

The most important investor to understand this is Duan Yongping — BBK/VIVO founder, Huang Zheng's mentor, and holder of 7.75 million PDD shares ($906M). His Xueqiu commentary cuts through the noise: *"This is essentially the Costco model. If PDD's online brand has around 4,000 SKUs, in 10 years it will be quite formidable."* [E4]

Critically, Duan argues PDD **does not need to compete with its merchants**: *"PDD doesn't need to steal merchants' business — just nurture hit-product companies, supervise quality, and license its brand to qualified factories."* [E5] This is a brand-licensing model, not a private-label-competitor model. The distinction matters enormously for how we assess merchant conflict, margin structure, and competitive risk.

Our revised assessment: **Xinpinmu is strategically sound, structurally lighter than it appears, and the right comparison is Kirkland (not JD's distributor model or Amazon Basics).** The execution risk is real but manageable. The tariff crisis makes it necessary. The founding vision makes it inevitable.

## Why the JD Comparison Was Wrong

The most common analytical error — one we made in V1 — is comparing Xinpinmu to JD's self-operated business. This is structurally wrong. There are three distinct models for platform-owned products, and they have fundamentally different economics, merchant relationships, and risk profiles:

| Dimension | JD Self-Operated | Amazon Basics | Xinpinmu / Kirkland |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **What they do** | Buy Samsung TVs, resell them | Create batteries that undercut Duracell | Design product specs, license brand to factories |
| **Brand ownership** | Sells OTHER people's brands | Owns the brand, competes with sellers | Owns the brand, partners with factories |
| **Role** | Distributor / retailer | Private-label competitor | Brand creator / quality certifier |
| **Inventory** | Heavy — buys and holds stock | Medium — Amazon warehouses | Light — factories produce to order |
| **Gross margin** | 14-19% (distributor economics) | ~30% estimated | 20-35% (brand-owner economics) |
| **Merchant conflict** | HIGH — competes on same brands | HIGH — uses seller data against them | LOW — creates new brands, not copies |
| **Regulatory risk** | Low | HIGH — FTC antitrust trial Oct 2026 | Low-medium |
| **Analogy** | Walmart buying from P&G | Walmart Great Value undercutting P&G | Costco Kirkland partnering with P&G factories |

[E6] [E7] [E8]

JD's self-operated business runs at 14-19% gross margins because JD is a distributor — it buys branded inventory, holds it, and resells at thin markups. JD Jingzao (京造) exists but is a tiny side project with undisclosed revenue. [E9]

Xinpinmu is not a distributor. It is a brand owner that contracts with manufacturing cluster factories. The correct margin benchmarks are:

| Brand-Owner Comparable | Gross Margin | Operating Margin | Revenue Scale |
|----------------------|-------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Anker Innovations | 44.6% | 9.4% | RMB 29.3B TTM |
| UGREEN | 37.4% | 8.7% | RMB 6.2B |
| Xiaomi IoT/Lifestyle | 23-25% | Improving | RMB 123B |
| Kirkland Signature | ~15% (capped) | N/A (Costco bundles) | $58-90B |
| Amazon 3P Private Label | 30-50% | ~20%+ | Fragmented |

[E10] [E11] [E12] [E13]

Xinpinmu's margin will depend on pricing strategy. If PDD prices aggressively (Kirkland-style, 20% below brands): ~20% gross margin. If PDD positions mid-tier (Xiaomi ecosystem): ~25%. If PDD goes premium DTC (Anker-lite): ~35%. The correct modeling range is 20-35%, not JD's 14-19%.

## The Founding Vision, Fulfilled

### Huang Zheng's "Costco + Disney"

Xinpinmu did not emerge from a vacuum. It traces directly to Huang Zheng's 2018 IPO letter:

> *"It will be a Costco and Disney driven by distributed intelligent agent networks."* [E1]

By 2023, PDD had quietly shifted from "多实惠 + 多乐趣" (multi-value + multi-fun) to "多实惠 + 好服务" (multi-value + good service), deprioritizing the Disney component. [E14] Xinpinmu is the Costco component coming to life — curated selection, quality certification, platform-backed trust.

### The C2M Evolution

PDD's relationship with China's manufacturing clusters has deepened through four distinct phases:

| Phase | Period | Initiative | What PDD Did |
|-------|--------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | 2018 | New Brand Plan 1.0 | Support 1,000 factory partners |
| 2 | 2020 | New Brand Plan 2.0 + 百亿补贴 | 5,000 partners, RMB 1T sales target |
| 3 | 2022-2025 | Temu + 新质供给 | International expansion + factory teams in 100+ clusters |
| 4 | 2026 | Xinpinmu | PDD OWNS the brand, licenses to factories |

[E15] [E16]

Each phase built capabilities for the next. By Phase 3, PDD had already embedded specialized teams in 100+ manufacturing clusters through the "新质供给" (New Quality Supply) program — the same clusters Xinpinmu now targets. The results were measurable: average order value increased 47%, repeat purchase rate rose from 23% to 40%. [E17]

**Xinpinmu is not PDD learning to work with factories. PDD already knows how. Xinpinmu is PDD deciding to own the brand.**

### Why Now — Seven Converging Forces

The timing is not arbitrary. Seven factors converged in Q1 2026:

1. **Revenue growth collapsed**: 131% (Q1 2024) → 10% (FY2025) — needs new growth engine [E18]
2. **Domestic users plateaued**: 872M MAU, only +3M in two years — organic ceiling reached [E19]
3. **De minimis tariffs killed**: Temu's cross-border model demolished — needs brand + local warehousing [E20]
4. **RMB 422B cash pile**: Needs productive deployment; market punishing PDD with 10.5x P/E for hoarding [E21]
5. **Cost structure already shifting**: FY2025 costs +23% vs revenue +10%, R&D +30% — reinvestment phase begun [E22]
6. **Leadership mandate**: Zhao Jiazhen became co-CEO Dec 2025 with explicit mandate to "rebuild PDD in 3 years" [E23]
7. **Regulatory tailwinds**: New Chinese rules (Oct 2025, Apr 2026) ban platform price coercion — pushing toward value creation over extraction [E24]

## How Kirkland Actually Works (And How Xinpinmu Adapts It)

### The Kirkland Operating Model

Kirkland Signature is the gold standard: $58-90B in annual sales from roughly 600 SKUs, representing 23% of Costco's revenue from just 12.5% of its assortment. [E13] [E25]

Key operational details:

- **Supplier partnerships**: Costco does NOT own factories. It contracts with premium national-brand manufacturers. Starbucks roasts Kirkland coffee. Duracell makes Kirkland batteries. Kimberly-Clark produces Kirkland diapers. The factory produces for BOTH the national brand AND Kirkland. [E26]
- **Quality control**: Costco's CEO personally signs off on each new Kirkland item. Products must match or exceed national brand quality at 20% lower prices. Categories where national brands cannot be matched are abandoned (cosmetics, soda, toothpaste). [E27]
- **Pricing discipline**: Maximum 14% markup on national brands, 15% on Kirkland. The margin premium comes from cutting out brand marketing costs, not from charging more. [E28]
- **Trust**: 30 years of consistent quality delivery. Members trust Kirkland because Costco has never compromised — the membership fee model aligns incentives. [E29]
- **ROIC**: 33.7% return on invested capital vs. Walmart's 17.1%. $850K revenue per employee vs. $348K industry average. [E30]

### Xinpinmu's Adaptation

Xinpinmu adapts this model for a digital platform:

| Kirkland | Xinpinmu Adaptation |
|----------|-------------------|
| Physical stores with curated shelves | Algorithm-driven product recommendations with curated selection |
| ~600 Kirkland SKUs in ~3,700 total | ~4,000 branded SKUs across categories |
| Costco buys inventory, holds in warehouse | Factories produce to PDD spec; PDD brand-licenses |
| Membership fee aligns incentives | Platform data + repeat purchase loop builds trust |
| 30 years of trust-building | Leverage existing 872M-user platform + 100+ cluster relationships |
| US + 13 countries | 90+ countries via Temu from day one |

The critical insight from Duan Yongping: 4,000 SKUs covers approximately 80% of high-frequency household consumption needs, creating a consumption closed loop. [E31] This transforms user behavior from multi-platform price comparison to single-platform trust — exactly what Costco achieved physically.

## The Brand-Licensing Model: Complementary, Not Competitive

### Duan Yongping's Framework

Duan Yongping's interpretation of how Xinpinmu will work is the most important investor framework for understanding the merchant relationship:

> *"拼多多不需要抢客户生意，只要扶植爆品公司就好，监督质量，达到标准就可以把自己的品牌授权给厂家。只需要选出某些有代表性的产品用来打自己的品牌就可以了。"*
>
> "PDD doesn't need to steal merchants' business — just nurture hit-product companies, supervise quality, and if they meet standards, license the brand to factories. It only needs to select certain representative products to build its own brand." [E5]

This describes a **per-SKU authorization model**: PDD handles standardized products (标品) under the Xinpinmu brand; existing merchants handle non-standard, personalized, and long-tail products (非标). This is a structural division of labor, not a zero-sum competition. [E32]

### Kirkland Proves Dual-Manufacturing Works

Costco's supplier model validates this approach. National brand manufacturers simultaneously produce:
- Their own branded products (sold at brand pricing)
- Kirkland products (sold at 20% discount, same quality)

Duracell, Starbucks, Kimberly-Clark, and dozens of major manufacturers all operate this dual model profitably. The factory's total volume increases even as the brand's pricing power on the branded product may modestly decline. [E26]

### The 新质供给 Foundation

Xinpinmu is not starting cold. PDD's "New Quality Supply" (新质供给) program has been running since April 2025, with teams embedded in 100+ manufacturing clusters. Documented outcomes: [E17]

- **Shaodong luggage**: Factories producing 70%+ of China's student backpacks transitioned from pure OEM. "Cooleni" brand emerged with PDD's digital support.
- **Yiwu cosmetics**: 170+ factories, with PDD teams improving formulation and packaging standards.
- **Weihai fishing gear**: Already producing 60%+ of the world's fishing rods; Handing brand doing RMB 300M+ on Amazon.

These are factories that WANT platform-backed brand support. They lack marketing expertise, international certification knowledge, and brand development resources. Xinpinmu provides exactly what they're missing.

### The Honest Risk

The complementary framing is the design intent. But execution always introduces tension:

- Even Kirkland displaces some national brands (Kind bars, Planters nuts, Pampers diapers have all lost shelf space). [E33]
- On standardized products, Xinpinmu WILL take share from existing PDD merchants who sell unbranded versions. [E34]
- Chinese media warns: "PDD's small/medium merchants face the biggest impact" on commoditized categories. [E35]
- PDD's traffic algorithm could preference Xinpinmu products — the "referee and athlete" concern. [E36]

The question is whether the overall ecosystem grows (factory volumes up, consumer trust up, platform stickiness up) enough to offset category-level displacement. Costco's 40-year track record suggests yes — but PDD is attempting this transition much faster.

## The Tariff Survival Engine

### The Old Temu Model is Dead

Xinpinmu is not just a growth strategy — it is a survival mechanism for Temu. The cross-border per-parcel model has been demolished:

**United States** (de minimis revoked May 2025):
- $100 per-parcel postal fee on every shipment from China [E20]
- 54% tariff on product value (current trade-truce rate)
- A $10 product now costs $115.40 via the old model: product ($10) + air freight ($7) + tariff ($5.40) + per-parcel fee ($100) [E37]

**European Union** (effective July 1, 2026):
- EUR 3 flat duty per item category on all parcels under EUR 150 [E38]
- EUR 2 additional handling fee from November 2026
- Full standard tariffs from 2028

### The New Value Chain

Xinpinmu restructures the entire value chain:

| Step | Old Temu Model | Xinpinmu Model |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Production | Factory makes generic product | Factory makes to Xinpinmu spec, PDD-branded |
| Shipping | Individual air parcel ($6-10/pkg) | Bulk ocean container ($0.10-0.30/kg) |
| Customs | Per-parcel entry + $100 fee | Formal bulk entry, standard 25% duty, no per-parcel fee |
| Warehousing | None (direct to consumer) | Local warehouse (13 self-owned: 10 EU, 2 US, 1 Mexico) |
| Last mile | International tracking, 7-15 days | Local carrier, 2-5 days |
| Landed cost ($10 product) | ~$122.40 | ~$16.00 |

[E37] [E39] [E40]

The cost reduction is staggering: **~87% lower landed cost** by switching from per-parcel air freight to bulk ocean + local fulfillment. Temu itself reports 40-60% logistics cost reduction from bulk shipping to UK warehouses alone. [E41]

### But Xinpinmu Adds New Costs

This is not a free lunch. PDD is taking on costs it has never borne:

- **Inventory risk**: PDD was 100% third-party marketplace. Xinpinmu requires pre-purchasing and holding stock. [E42]
- **Working capital**: RMB 15B first tranche, RMB 100B over three years. [E2]
- **Brand development**: Product design, packaging, IP protection, quality certification. [E43]
- **Organizational build**: PDD's 17,000 staff must add designers, quality inspectors, warehouse operators, brand marketers, IP lawyers. [E42]

Chinese media describes this as "搬砖类型的苦活累活" — heavy brick-carrying work, a fundamental departure from PDD's asset-light DNA. [E42]

### The Pricing Power Thesis

Higher-value Xinpinmu-branded products dilute tariff impact as a percentage of price. A $12 branded fishing rod absorbs a 25% tariff ($3) more easily than a $5 generic product ($1.25) — and the branded product commands higher absolute margins. [E44]

Duan Yongping's "online Costco" framing matters here: if consumers trust the Xinpinmu brand the way Costco members trust Kirkland, PDD can price above generic Temu but below established brands — the exact sweet spot that maximizes volume × margin.

V1's scenario analysis used JD's 14-19% gross margin. This was wrong — JD is a distributor, not a brand owner. The correct benchmarks:

[E10] [E11] [E12] [E45]

Xinpinmu becomes clearly accretive to PDD's valuation at approximately 22% gross margin with RMB 25B+ revenue. [E45]

### Blended Margin Impact

If Xinpinmu reaches 10% of PDD's revenue at 22% gross margin, the company-wide blended gross margin drops from ~57% to ~53%. This looks like margin compression — but it's not necessarily value-destructive. [E45]

Consider: Amazon's gross margin compressed from 33% to 27% as AWS scaled from 2015-2018. The stock quadrupled. Revenue quality matters more than margin percentage. Branded product revenue is more durable than advertising revenue, which depends on merchant willingness to bid for traffic.

### Option Value Framework

The market assigns Xinpinmu approximately zero value today:

- PDD enterprise value: ~$85B
- Net income: $15.4B → 5.6x EV/NI
- Cash: RMB 422B ($60B) = 56% of market cap [E46]

If Xinpinmu generates $3-5B annual operating profit at maturity, it would be worth $30-75B at 10-15x — representing 35-86% upside to current enterprise value. [E47]

The asymmetry is favorable: downside is manageable margin compression (RMB 100B = 24% of cash, fundable from FCF without touching reserves). Upside is a new branded business with global distribution.

## The Tension That Matters: 3 Years vs. 10 Years

Duan Yongping says 10 years. Management says 3 years. This is the most important tension in the entire thesis.

Costco spent its first decade (1983-1993) not aggressively expanding but polishing ~4,000 SKUs — compressing costs, stabilizing quality, building trust. [E54] Duan's 10-year framework maps to this: *"it's not about the time itself but emphasizing that quality business models need time to be forged."* [E54]

Management's three-year, RMB 100B commitment suggests urgency — driven by the tariff crisis forcing Temu's model transformation. The tension:

- **3-year timeline**: forces rapid execution, risks quality shortcuts, creates merchant anxiety
- **10-year vision**: allows patient brand-building, trust accumulation, category expansion

The resolution may be that RMB 100B over 3 years builds the **infrastructure** (factory relationships, warehouse network, quality systems, initial SKU portfolio), while brand trust accumulates over the subsequent 7 years. The investment front-loads; the returns back-load.

## Kill Conditions (Revised)

Three observable events that would invalidate the thesis:

1. **PDD traffic-preferences Xinpinmu products in search results** — this would confirm the "referee and athlete" concern, trigger regulatory action under new Chinese pricing rules, and echo Amazon's antitrust problems. Watch for: merchant complaints about algorithmic bias, regulatory investigations.

2. **Xinpinmu gross margins below 15% after 18 months** — would indicate PDD is running a distributor model (JD-style), not a brand-owner model. The entire thesis depends on brand-owner economics (20%+).

3. **Duan Yongping reduces his PDD position** — his endorsement is the strongest signal of strategic conviction from someone who understands both PDD's culture and the Costco model. A sell would signal loss of confidence in execution.
