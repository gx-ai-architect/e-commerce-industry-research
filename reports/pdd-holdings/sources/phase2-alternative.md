## 2.6 Regulatory Timeline

### US De Minimis / Section 321 Reform

| Date | Action | Impact |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Feb 4, 2025** | Suspension of de minimis for China/Hong Kong goods | First targeted action; reduced volumes 30-35% |
| **Apr 2, 2025** | Executive Order 14257: National emergency over trade deficits | Legal foundation for broader restrictions |
| **May 2, 2025** | De minimis exemption eliminated for China/HK (enforcement begins) | Temu cross-border parcels now subject to 54% tariff or $100 flat fee |
| **Jul 4, 2025** | "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" passed by Congress | Permanently repeals Section 321 effective July 1, 2027; civil penalties up to $10,000 for order-splitting |
| **Jul 30, 2025** | Executive Order 14324: Global suspension of de minimis | Applies to ALL countries, effective Aug 29 |
| **Aug 3, 2025** | Civil fines for de minimis misuse take effect | $5,000 first violation, $10,000 repeat |
| **Aug 29, 2025** | Global de minimis suspension enforced | All packages >$0 subject to duties; only bona fide gifts <$100 exempt |
| **Feb 28, 2026** | Flat-rate postal duty option expires | Only ad valorem rates apply; tied to Section 122 surcharge framework |
| **Jul 1, 2027** | Permanent statutory repeal of Section 321 | Codified by One Big Beautiful Bill Act |

**Processing impact:** Formerly exempt shipments went from 2-4 hour processing to 2-4 days
  - Source: [CBP Guidance](https://content.govdelivery.com/accounts/USDHSCBP/bulletins/3f01456)

### EU Enforcement Actions

**Digital Services Act (DSA):**
- **July 29, 2025:** European Commission preliminarily found Temu in breach of DSA obligations regarding illegal product risks
- Mystery shopping exercise found consumers "very likely to find non-compliant products" (baby toys, small electronics)
- Temu's October 2024 risk assessment deemed "inaccurate," relying on "general industry information" rather than platform-specific data
- Ongoing investigation into: addictive design features, recommendation system transparency, researcher data access, mitigation measure effectiveness
- **Potential fine:** Up to 6% of global annual turnover
- **Precedent:** Commission imposed EUR 120 million fine on X (formerly Twitter) for DSA violations in December 2025 — first DSA non-compliance decision
  - Source: [European Sting](https://europeansting.com/2025/07/29/commission-preliminarily-finds-temu-in-breach-of-the-digital-services-act-in-relation-to-illegal-products-on-its-platform/)
