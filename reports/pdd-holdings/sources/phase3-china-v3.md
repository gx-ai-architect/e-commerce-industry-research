  - Source: https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202512/31/WS69547f25a310d6866eb3159c.html

## 3.4 C2M and Factory Ecosystem

### Pinduoduo C2M Model

- **Core concept:** Aggregates consumer demand data, shares with upstream suppliers for tailored production. Bypasses brand-distributor chain: market demand -> platform algorithm -> factory production scheduling -> consumers. [DATE: ongoing]
  - Source: https://fourweekmba.com/consumer-to-manufacturer-c2m/

- **New Brand Initiative** (launched Dec 2018): Supports 1,000+ factories to develop own brands. Provides user insights, logistical support, preferential placement, livestreaming. [DATE: 2018-ongoing]
  - Source: https://alexandre.substack.com/p/-look-east-not-west-the-rise-of-c2m

- **December 2025:** Co-Chairman Zhao Jiazhen announced plan to "recreate a Pinduoduo" by "heavily investing in China's supply chain" over next 3 years. Leverages global market demand pool covering 90+ countries. [DATE: 2025-12]
  - Source: https://techbuzzchina.substack.com/p/temus-phase-3-building-a-new-pinduoduo

- **100-billion-yuan merchant support initiative (2025):** Includes fee reductions, subsidies for SME sellers, cross-border support. [DATE: 2025]
  - Source: https://www.ainvest.com/news/pinduoduo-merchant-support-gambit-short-term-cost-long-term-commerce-edge-2507/

### China Factory Capacity Utilization

- **Full-year 2025:** Industrial capacity utilization rate for manufacturing was 74.6%. [DATE: 2025]
- **Q1 2025:** 74.1% (up from 73.8% in Q1 2024). [DATE: 2025-Q1]
- **Q2 2025:** 74.0% (lowest since Q1 2020). [DATE: 2025-Q2]

  - Source: https://www.dallasfed.org/research/economics/2025/1230

- **2026 outlook:** Manufacturing output projected to grow 3.6%. Capacity reduction plans expected to ease overcapacity. Industrial production growth likely to ease as domestic economy is soft and export growth fades. [DATE: 2026 projected]

## 3.5 Macro Context

### Consumer Spending Recovery

- **GDP growth projections:** 4.5% in 2025, 4.0% in 2026 (World Bank). [DATE: 2025-2026]
  - Source: https://www.worldbank.org/en/news/press-release/2025/06/13/unlocking-consumption-to-sustain-growth-in-china-world-bank-economic-update

- **Consumer Confidence Index:** Near historic lows, only gradually recovering. [DATE: 2025]
- **Personal savings rate:** Above 30% since 2020. Net new household savings deposits in H1 2025: 17.94 trillion RMB (up from 11.46 trillion in H1 2024). [DATE: 2025-H1]
  - Source: https://www.mckinsey.com/cn/our-insights/our-insights/mid-year-update-five-surprises-from-chinas-consumer-market

- **Retail sales H1 2025:** +5% YoY, exceeding expectations. May 2025 was particularly strong at +6.4%. [DATE: 2025-H1]
  - Source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202507/t20250730_1960541.html

- **Per capita consumption expenditure H1 2025:** 14,309 yuan (real increase +5.3% after deducting price factors). [DATE: 2025-H1]

- **Weak Q4 2025:** Retail sales +0.9% YoY in Dec 2025, slowest since Dec 2022. [DATE: 2025-12]

- **Service vs goods:** Service consumption is "poised to become the primary engine for consumption growth" (Huatai Securities). Service retail sales grew 5.1% in first 8 months of 2025, faster than goods. [DATE: 2025]
  - Source: https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202510/1345968.shtml

- **IMF recommendation:** Pivoting to consumption-led growth is the "overarching policy priority." [DATE: 2026-02]
  - Source: https://www.imf.org/en/news/articles/2026/02/18/cf-how-chinas-economy-can-pivot-to-consumption-led-growth

### China Retail Sales

- **Full-year 2025:** Retail sales of consumer goods +3.7% YoY, totaling 50.12 trillion yuan (~$7.15 trillion). [DATE: 2025]
  - Source: https://english.news.cn/20260119/5e88d6bfa8664575b31da539ae2615ef/c.html

- **Online retail sales 2025:** +8.6% YoY to 15.97 trillion yuan. Physical goods online: +5.2% to 13.09 trillion yuan (26.1% of total retail). [DATE: 2025]

- **Urban vs rural:** Urban retail +3.6%, rural +4.1% YoY. [DATE: 2025]

- **Final consumption contribution to GDP:** 52%, up 5 percentage points from prior year. [DATE: 2025]
  - Source: https://www.stats.gov.cn/english/PressRelease/202601/t20260120_1962354.html

- **Early 2026 (Jan-Feb):** Retail sales +2.8% YoY (beat 2.5% forecast), up from 0.9% in Dec 2025. Strong in food (+10.2%), clothing (+10.4%). Autos still weak (-7.3%). [DATE: 2026-01/02]
  - Source: https://www.scmp.com/economy/economic-indicators/article/3346697/chinas-economy-starts-2026-strongly-retail-sales-investment-rise

- **2026 GDP target:** 4.5-5%, the least ambitious since early 1990s. [DATE: 2026]

### Consumption Downgrade vs Upgrade

- **Downgrade evidence:**
  - Hakuhodo 2024 survey: 80% of Chinese consumers opted for cheaper products/services. Consumption willingness index fell to 67.3 (-9.1% from 2019). [DATE: 2024]
  - Luxury consumption contracted 20% in 2024 (Bain & Company). [DATE: 2024]
  - CPI below 1% for 28 consecutive months since May 2023. [DATE: 2025]
  - Housing prices in 70 major cities down 10.4% from peak, falling for 41 consecutive months. [DATE: 2025]
  - Source: https://www.thinkingtaiwan.net/article/100119

- **Upgrade/nuance evidence:**
  - Total retail sales exceeded 50 trillion yuan. NEV, smart devices, cultural/tourism experiences surging. [DATE: 2025]
  - "Buy expensive, but don't overpay" — young consumers justify premiums for feel-good spending (pet accessories, collectible toys) while rationalizing everyday purchases. [DATE: 2025]
  - Service consumption average annual growth 9.6% (2020-2024). [DATE: 2020-2024]
  - Source: https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202510/1345968.shtml
  - Source: https://english.news.cn/20251224/4d1e68f394df45808154c76f52b6d58a/c.html

- **2026 outlook:** "The recovery is structural, not cyclical." Consumers won't return to 2019 "status-seeking" splurge. Trading up in frequency/experience, trading down in unit price. [DATE: 2026]
  - Source: https://hubofchina.com/chinas-2026-holiday-spending-trends/

- **Structural barriers to consumption recovery:**
  - 60-65% of total savings held by top 10% of households. [DATE: ongoing]
  - Housing = 59.1% of total household wealth. [DATE: ongoing]
  - Eight interlocking challenges: excess capacity, declining investment, weakening confidence, capital flight, property downturn, widening inequality, inadequate social welfare, demographic decline. [DATE: 2025]
  - Source: https://rhg.com/research/no-quick-fixes-chinas-long-term-consumption-growth/

- **Policy direction:** 15th Five-Year Plan (2026-30) prioritizes increasing consumption as growth driver. Fiscal policy should prioritize social protection (healthcare, pensions, unemployment) to reduce precautionary saving. [DATE: 2026]
  - Source: https://www.imf.org/en/news/articles/2026/02/18/cf-how-chinas-economy-can-pivot-to-consumption-led-growth

## Notes on Data Limitations

1. **QuestMobile paywalled:** Specific 2025-2026 DAU/MAU breakdowns for Pinduoduo are behind QuestMobile's paywall. Figures cited are from English-language summaries and secondary sources.
2. **CNNIC reports:** Full PDF reports are in Chinese. Data cited is from English-language press releases and summaries.
3. **GMV figures vary widely** by source due to different definitions (B2C only vs. comprehensive, domestic vs. cross-border).
4. **Douyin financials:** ByteDance is private, so Douyin e-commerce figures come from leaked/reported data via 36Kr and other media outlets.
5. **Market share data:** The most recent comprehensive breakdown is from 2024. 2025 full-year breakdowns are not yet publicly available.
