## 1.5 Key Risk Factors

### VIE Structure Risk

**Structure:** PDD Holdings Inc. is a Cayman Islands holding company. Holders of ADSs do NOT have direct or indirect equity interests in the VIE or its subsidiaries. The company maintains only contractual arrangements with the VIE, which allow it to consolidate financial results under U.S. GAAP.

**Cash restrictions:** Cash held by the VIE and its subsidiaries, as well as mainland China subsidiaries, may not be available to fund operations outside of mainland China due to regulatory restrictions on currency conversion, cross-border transactions, and cross-border capital flows.

**Funding limitations:** Under PRC law, PDD Holdings may provide funding to its mainland China subsidiaries only through capital contributions or loans, and to the VIE only through loans, subject to government registration and approval requirements.

**Restricted net assets in China:** $14.3 billion as of FY2024.

Source: [PDD 20-F FY2024](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1737806/000141057825000951/pdd-20241231x20f.htm)

### Regulatory Approval Risk (CSRC)

Under the M&A Rules, a special purpose vehicle formed for listing purposes through acquisition of mainland China companies and controlled by mainland China persons must obtain CSRC approval before listing outside China. Failure to obtain such approval could result in fines, delays in repatriation of proceeds, restrictions on ability to remain listed on a U.S. exchange, or other material adverse actions.

Source: [PDD 20-F FY2024](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1737806/000141057825000951/pdd-20241231x20f.htm)

### Tariff & Trade Policy Risk (De Minimis)

- Trump ended the de minimis exemption (packages under $800 duty-free) effective May 2, 2025
- Temu lost **58% of daily U.S. visitors** after the de minimis exemption ended
- Temu's strategic response: shifting to U.S. and European warehouses for ~25% of inventory
- Citi estimated Temu's local warehouse GMV may have contributed 20%+ of U.S. GMV by end-2024
- PDD stock fell sharply on February 3, 2025 when tariff closure was announced

Source: [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/02/03/temu-parent-pdds-stock-tumbles-as-trump-tariffs-close-trade-loophole.html), [PYMNTS](https://www.pymnts.com/earnings/2025/temu-parent-pdd-holdings-reports-7percent-revenue-growth-despite-tariffs)

### Competition Risk

- Revenue growth decelerated from 59% (FY2024) to single digits (7-10% in 2025 quarters)
- Management repeatedly cites "intense competition" and "evolving competitive landscape"
- Non-GAAP operating margin fell from 36% (Q2 2024) to 27% (Q2 2025) — a 9pp compression
- Domestic competition from JD.com, Alibaba, Douyin (TikTok) e-commerce
- International competition from Shein, Amazon, and local players in each market

### Securities Class Action Lawsuits

**Case:** Baxter v. PDD Holdings Inc., No. 24-cv-05653 (E.D.N.Y.)
- Filed August 13, 2024 by Levi & Korsinsky
- Class period: April 30, 2021 to June 25, 2024 (expanded to Sept 12, 2024 by Rosen Law)
- Alleges violations of Securities Exchange Act Sections 10(b) and 20(a), Rule 10b-5
- **Key allegations:** PDD made false/misleading statements about whether its apps contained malware, including claims that the Pinduoduo app exploited Android vulnerabilities to install backdoors, gain unauthorized data access, bypass security to monitor other apps, read private messages, and change settings
- Also alleges failure to disclose risk of goods made by forced labor on the platform

Source: [Nasdaq](https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/class-action-lawsuit-against-pdd-holdings-nasdaq-pdd), [GlobeNewsWire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2024/10/15/2963418/0/en/PDD-Investors-Have-Opportunity-to-Lead-PDD-Holdings-Inc-Securities-Fraud-Lawsuit-with-the-Schall-Law-Firm.html)

### Arkansas Attorney General Lawsuit

## Summary: Key Financial Narratives

1. **Explosive growth era ending:** Revenue growth decelerated from 90% (FY2023) to 59% (FY2024) to single digits (7-10% in 2025). The Temu-fueled hypergrowth phase is over.

2. **Deliberate margin compression:** Operating profit declined 38% YoY in Q1 2025 and 21% in Q2 2025 as PDD pours capital into merchant support (RMB 100B program), fee reductions (RMB 10B), and logistics infrastructure. This is framed as a strategic choice, not a structural decline.

3. **Massive cash fortress:** $59.5B in cash/short-term investments as of Q3 2025 — more than 40% of market cap. Net cash of ~$58B or ~$41/share. No meaningful debt. No dividends. No buyback program announced.

4. **Tariff headwind is real:** De minimis exemption removal on May 2, 2025 led to 58% decline in Temu's U.S. daily visitors. Company is responding with local warehouse strategy but the shift is early.

5. **Deep value territory:** Trading at 10.5x trailing P/E vs. 19x 10-year median. EV/EBITDA of 6.6x. If you back out net cash, the operating business trades at ~6x earnings. Consensus target implies 35% upside.

6. **Legal/regulatory overhang:** Active securities class action, Arkansas AG data privacy lawsuit, VIE structure risk, CSRC approval risk, and ongoing data security scrutiny.
