## 3. China Regulatory Environment

### SAMR Antitrust Legislative Agenda (2026)

- SAMR announced it will step up antitrust enforcement, rein in "cutthroat competition," and better develop the platform economy as 2026 priorities. Focus on dismantling administrative monopolies and deepening fair competition governance. [DATE: 2026-01]
  - Source: https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202512/31/WS69547f25a310d6866eb3159c.html
  - Source: https://www.bricscompetition.org/news/chinas-samr-unveils-expansive-antitrust-legislative-agenda-ahead-of-2026

### Draft Antitrust Guidelines for Internet Platforms (Nov 2025)

- SAMR released draft antitrust compliance guidelines addressing eight categories of monopoly risks: algorithmic collusion, below-cost sales, forced exclusivity ("pick one of two"), unfair pricing, blocking/delisting rivals. Public feedback period closed end of November 2025. [DATE: 2025-11]
  - Source: https://english.www.gov.cn/news/202511/18/content_WS691be124c6d00ca5f9a07a26.html

### Ban on Forced "Lowest Price" Practices (Dec 2025)

- Joint regulation by NDRC, SAMR, and CAC (29 articles) banning platform operators from leveraging dominant scale to impose "lowest price" agreements. Prohibits traffic throttling, search ranking demotions, or algorithm penalties to pressure merchants. **Published Dec 9, 2025; effective Apr 10, 2026; valid through Apr 10, 2031.** [DATE: 2025-12-09]
  - Source: https://www.yahoo.com/news/articles/china-bans-e-commerce-platforms-093000593.html
  - Source: https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202512/20/WS69466382a310d6866eb2fa85.html

### Pricing Law Revisions

- NDRC and SAMR released draft revisions to the Pricing Law banning platform companies from forcing retailers to sell below cost. Expected to become law **Q1 2026 at earliest**. [DATE: 2025-07]
  - Source: https://triviumchina.com/2025/07/25/revisions-to-pricing-law-target-e-commerce-companies/

### Revised Anti-Unfair Competition Law (AUCL)

- Enacted **June 27, 2025**, effective **October 15, 2025** — most significant update since 1993. Key features: [DATE: 2025-06-27]
  - E-commerce platforms cannot coerce merchants into selling below cost or restrict them from rival platforms
  - Extraterritorial reach: applies to unfair competition conducted outside China if it disrupts domestic market
  - Fines up to RMB 5 million, with personal liability for key individuals
  - Source: https://www.quarles.com/newsroom/publications/chinas-sweeping-2025-anti-unfair-competition-law-reform-far-reaching-impacts-on-brand-protection-data-governance-ecommerce-and-platform-regulation

## Key Takeaways for V2 Report

1. **Market position strengthening:** PDD's China market share has grown from ~19% (mid-2023) to ~23% (early 2026), while Alibaba declined from 44% to 32%.

2. **Revenue growth decelerating, margins compressing:** Q1 2025 revenue +10% but operating profit -38%; Q2 2025 revenue +7% but operating profit -21%. Heavy investment cycle in merchant support and instant retail.

3. **Douyin is the primary competitive threat:** GMV growing 30%+ annually, approaching 4T yuan. Shelf e-commerce now 50% of Douyin's GMV. Expected to become #1 in global online apparel by 2026.

4. **JD food delivery adds new competitive dimension:** 157% revenue surge but massive losses (RMB 46.6B). 2026 will be pivotal for unit economics.

5. **Regulatory environment becoming more structured:** Shift from "centralized rectification" to "rule-building." New pricing rules (effective Apr 2026) ban forced lowest-price practices — could reduce pressure on Pinduoduo's margin-eroding price war strategy.

6. **Macro headwinds persist:** Flat CPI in 2025, record household savings, soft consumer confidence. But Pinduoduo's value proposition is well-suited to price-conscious consumers.

7. **Instant retail is the next growth vector:** RMB 10B+ investment commitment, targeting 20% market share in 2–3 years.

8. **Agriculture/C2M remains a strategic moat:** Largest D2C agriculture platform, 16M+ farmers connected, government-aligned agricultural modernization narrative.
