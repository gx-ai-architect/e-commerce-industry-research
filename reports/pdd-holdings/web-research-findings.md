## 2. Temu Unit Economics Per Order

### Average Order Value (AOV)
- **Q4 2023**: $38.9 (+62.08% YoY)
- **Current US Range**: $35-50
- **Target US AOV**: $50 (comparable to Shein)
- **General Range**: $30-50 for department store products

### Shipping Costs
- **Typical Order Cost Breakdown**: $32 total, including $8-9 in logistics costs
- **Subsidy Structure**: 30% subsidies maintained (consistent with 2023)

### Marketing & Subsidies
- **2024 Marketing Spend**: $4.3 billion total
- **2024 Subsidy Spend**: $1.3 billion (discounts)
- **Free/Low-Cost Shipping**: Heavily subsidized, often resulting in losses per order

### Profitability Challenge
- **Loss Rate**: Overall ~40%, improved to 30-35% in US
- **CAC vs AOV**: Customer acquisition costs now exceed AOV in US (critical metric)
- **Highest Loss Components**: Marketing expenses and logistics

## 3. Competitive Pricing Analysis

### 2026 Controlled Price Study
- **Study Design**: Multi-city price audit over 6 weeks (early 2026), ordering identical items across 12 categories in US, Canada, Germany, Australia
- **Measurement**: Total landed cost (shipping + customs + currency conversion + restocking penalties)
- **Order Window**: March 4-15, 2026 (non-promotional periods)

### Key Findings
- **General Winner**: Temu offers lowest prices due to direct-from-supplier structure and aggressive subsidies
- **Shein**: Slightly higher prices but competitive in fast-fashion
- **Canadian Example** (5 uniform items):
  - Temu: $84.62 total
  - Shein: $106.82 total (26% more expensive)
  - Temu refund: 48 hours to debit card

### Platform Positioning
- **Temu**: Sells many items at/below cost; wins on single items or non-fashion goods
- **Shein**: Better for clothing hauls due to coupons/rewards
- **Amazon Haul**: Products under $20 (some as low as $1); 5% off $50+, 10% off $75+

### Market Performance (2025 Australia)
- **Amazon**: $7.1 billion
- **Temu**: ~$3 billion
- **Shein**: ~$1.9 billion
